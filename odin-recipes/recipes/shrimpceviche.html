Shrimp Ceviche

Fresh is the key to this delightful appetizer!

My friends absolutely love it and beg me for the recipe! You can always double it for larger parties--it goes FAST! Serve as a dip with tortilla chips or as a topping on a tostada spread with mayo. The fearless palate might like this with hot sauce.

Ingedients

  • 1 lb medium shrimp (peeled and deveined"
  • 1 cup fresh squeezed lime juice
  • 8 roma tomatoes (diced)
  • 1 yellow onion (diced)
  • 1 jalapeno pepper (minced)
  • 2 avacados (diced)*
  • 2 ribs celery (diced)*
  • chopped fresh cilantro to taste
  • salt and pepper to taste

* = optional

Directions

  1. Place shrimp in a glass bowl and cover with lime juice to marinate (or 'cook') for at least 1 hour, or until they turn pink and opaque. Meanwhile, place the roma tomatoes, onion and jalapeno (and avocados and celery, if using) in a large, non-reactive (stainless steel, glass or plastic) bowl.
  2. Remove shrimp from lime juice, reserving juice. Dice shrimp and add to the bowl of vegetables. Pour in the remaining lime juice marinade. Add cilantro and salt and pepper to taste. Toss gently to mix.
  3. Refrigerate for 30 minutes to 1 hour
  4. Remove from refrigerator, enjoy!
